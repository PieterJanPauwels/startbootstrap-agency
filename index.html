About

Open Source biodiversity technology

Started as a bird island

Our first case, birds.today is an island, located in the harbour site 'houtdok' specifically built to attract and provide space for the Common Tern.

It's IOT driven

The island is a totally self-sustained, with solar panels and batteries to power the IOT devices, and a 4G router to send through the biodiversity data and images.

Open Source by design

Everything from the technology on the island, the web tool developed up to the design of the island itself is Open Source and free to re-use.

OUR JOURNEY

How did we get there and what can you do?

  • March 2017

    Started out as a brainstorm idea

    Digipolis Gent was looking for an interesting use-case for IoT, aside from the known smart bins and mobility flows and ended up at the green service departement of the City of Ghent. They were keen to have more insights on waterbirds landing in the new harbour site and to have more insights on biodiversity in a city.

  • July 2017

    Then came #oSoc17 to build the technical part

    The idea to build and IoT island for birds was given to 5 Open Source coding students during Open Summer of Code. In just 16 days then went from a bag of hardware, to a full technical setup, webinterface and API to harvest biodiversity data.

  • August 2017

    And MEDS student came by to build the island

    MEDS or Meetings by Design Students was an international design workshop for architectural and design students that came together in Ghent. Four of these international students was given to the task to build this island and incorporate the hardware in just 10 days time.

  • September 2017

    Testing Period

    For 40 days, the installation was live and taking pictures of every bird landing on the island. The webinterface allowed citizens to check whether this was the right bird, a common tern, or not. Whenever a common tern was recognized by at least 5 participating citizens, this was translated into open biodiversity data.
